Saint-martin

Saint Martin's Island, located in the northeastern part of the Bay of Bengal, is the only coral island in Bangladesh. Known for its clear blue waters, sandy beaches, and vibrant coral reefs, it is a popular destination for both domestic and international tourists. The island is also known as "Narikel Jinjira," which means Coconut Island, due to the abundance of coconut trees.

Chimbuk

Chimbuk Hill, situated in the Bandarban district of Bangladesh, is the highest peak in the country, standing at approximately 2,592 feet above sea level. Known for its stunning panoramic views of the surrounding hills and valleys, Chimbuk Hill is a popular tourist destination in the Chittagong Hill Tracts region.

Sixty Dome Mosque

The Shat Gambuj Mosque, also known as the Sixty Dome Mosque, is an architectural marvel located in Bagerhat, a historic city in the southwestern part of Bangladesh. Constructed in the mid-15th century by Khan Jahan Ali, a Turkish general and saint, the mosque is a prime example of the Sultanate period's architecture. Despite its name, the mosque actually features 77 domes, including one dome over the central prayer hall.

Tea Garden

Srimangal in the Moulvibazar district can be called the tea capital of Bangladesh. The tea gardens of Srimangal cover about 450 sq-km area. It looks like a green carpet on the slope of the hill. Shatgaon tea garden has started in front of the 'Cha Konya (Tea Daughter)' sculpture at the entrance of Srimangal tea garden.
